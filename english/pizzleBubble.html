Pizzle Bubble

Prepa'ISIMA 2 project. A game based on the principle of Puzzle Bubble.

This project was developed as a team with two other individuals, Thomas Tamagnaud and Reynalde Segerie, as part of the end-of-year project for Prepa'ISIMA 2.

Our project manager, Lucas Pastor, proposed the idea of creating a bubble shooter game. For the name of the game, we chose "Pizzle Bubble" in reference to our dear Prepa'ISIMA students, affectionately nicknamed PIZZ, and as a nod to the game Puzzle Bubble. This game is accessible for one to three players, and even bots can participate. The goal is to solve puzzles by cleverly popping bubbles of matching colors.
